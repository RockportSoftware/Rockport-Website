Airport Scenario Simulator

Airports are constantly changing. The daily operation of an airport requires the coordination of thousands of employees from numerous organsations working towards and agreed schedule of flights and other transportation modes. Each day is different, with complex factors leading to continual schedule updates, delays or interruptions to services. The airport operator and its operating partners also need to make strategic planning decisions about future operations, which may include the introduction of new services, new facilties and improvements to the passenger experience.

Airports need a way to manage day to day operations as well as plan for the future, and rely on knowledge derived from every growing volumes of data from siloed airport applications and systems. The challenge for an airport is how to make effective use of this data to lead to informed decision making.

Rockport Software has developed a capability to simulate airport operations under a variety of scenarios in order support decision making. Airport resources are modelled so that anticipated demand can be simulated, with the optimum scenarios being selected for visualisation and verification.

Rockport Software is actively engaged at a major international airports designing and delivering AI solutions, including machine learning algorithms to predict passenger behaviour, assess the risk factors and probability that bags will miss their flights, optimising baggage handling system processes and delivering event driven simulations of airport operations.

ControlBEAM - Continuous Decision Intelligence

ControlBEAM, named for its Basic Entity Attribute Matrix event model, is an event-driven software framework developed with Rockport Software that provides a standard architecture for building interoperable, real-time digital systems. It is the technical foundation underpinning Rockport's digital twin, simulation, and AI/simulation work.

ControlBEAM - AI Ready Atomic Events

The central idea is the BEAM Event — an irreducible, time-stamped unit of information (an "atomic event") that carries identifiers for an entity's key information. Rather than systems communicating via complex, proprietary interfaces, all systems produce and consume these standardised atomic events. This makes previously siloed systems interoperable without custom integration work.

BEAM event identifiers are tied to a common ontology, which supports the metadata required to associate with events to provide the contextual relationships and other attributes required for data to be AI-ready.
